FAQs

How many drumettes per person?
As a main dish, plan about 8 drumettes per adult (about 6 to 7 for teens and 5 for children). For a mixed buffet, plan about 6 per adult. Appetizers are about 4 per adult.
How do you convert pieces to pounds?
We multiply the drumette count by your chosen size (about 1.5–2.5 oz each cooked) and divide by 16 to get pounds. We round to the nearest quarter-pound for easy shopping.
Do these counts include flats or just drumettes?
This calculator is tuned for drumettes only. If you are serving full wings (drumette + flat), use total pieces accordingly or ask for a full-wing version.
How much should I buy for 10, 25, 50, or 100 people?
For a main with typical appetite, rough guides are: 10 people about 80 drumettes, 25 people about 200, 50 people about 400, 100 people about 800. Use the calculator for your exact mix and size.
Does the estimate include sauce or sides?
Counts are for the drumettes themselves. Sauces and sides are separate. If your menu is side-heavy, pick “Mixed buffet” to scale portions down.

Quick notes

  • Per-adult baselines: Main about 8 pieces; Mixed about 6; Appetizer about 4. Teens about 0.8 adult; Children about 0.6 adult.
  • Size choices: Small about 1.5 oz cooked each; Medium about 2.0 oz; Large about 2.5 oz.
  • Rounding: We round pounds to the nearest quarter-pound.
  • Context multipliers: Appetite, event length, and leftovers can shift amounts by about 10–20%.
  • Disclaimer: Estimates only; adjust for your crowd and product size.
